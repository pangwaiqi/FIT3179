GLOBAL MORTALITY INSIGHTS

Global death encompasses mortality across the world, influenced by numerous factors, including healthcare access, diseases prevalence, socioeconomic, lifestyles, and environmental conditions.

We will also delve deeply into the pressing public health issue of suicides, highlighting its impact on individuals of all age groups, genders, and backgrounds.

The datasets have been sourced from Kaggle and WHO, comprising information on worldwide deaths and suicide-related deaths spanning the years 1950 to 2019.

The forthcoming statistics aim to provide comprehensive insights, fostering greater awareness.

SUICIDE DEATHS BY GENDER & AGE GROUPS FROM 1950 TO 2021

Let's proceed to visualize the suicides categorised by gender over the years. As illustrated in the chart below, suicides are more common among men. We can also observe that in recent years, suicides count have decreased significantly for males and slightly for females.

In the bubble chart presented, male suicides surpassed female suicides across all age groups, with a noteworthy concentration occurring between the ages of 20 to 69. This phenomenon could be associated with mental illnesses caused by occupational stress.

SUICIDE DEATHS VS MENTAL ILLNESSES

The following visualization illustrates the relationships between suicide rates and 5 distinct mental health conditions: depression, schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, eating disorders, and anxiety.

In the scatterplot matrix presented below, we observe that in recent years, there is a sudden spike in suicide rates in Lesotho, while its occurrences of each mental illness remain relatively consistent. We can also observe that the suicide rate decreased over the years for each mental illness.

Additionally, depression is the most common mental illness, followed by anxiety. Eating disorder is the least common mental illness.
